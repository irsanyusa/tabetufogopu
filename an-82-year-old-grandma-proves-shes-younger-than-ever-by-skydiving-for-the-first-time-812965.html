An 82-Year-Old Grandma Proves Shes Younger Than Ever by Skydiving for the First Time

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Saturday, June 8, 2024

Iluminada Fabroa is a true inspiration to people of all ages. At 82 years old, she became the oldest person to skydive in Siquijor, Philippines. Fabroa’s achievement shows that age is just a number and that we are never too old to pursue our passions and live life to the fullest.

It’s always inspiring to see people pursuing their dreams and challenging themselves, regardless of age or background. And Iluminada Fabroa is one of the best examples of that. Skydiving requires courage, physical strength, and mental agility, and for Fabroa to accomplish such a feat at an advanced age is truly remarkable.

Fabroa has a real thirst for adventure and a desire to push herself to new heights, both figuratively and literally. Her grandson mentioned that she has also climbed Mt. Apo, the Philippines’ highest peak, and gone cliff diving and canyoneering.

Iluminada Fabroa is not only a daring adventurer but also a practicing Certified Public Accountant and lawyer. It takes a great deal of discipline, dedication, and hard work to pursue such challenging careers, and it’s clear that Fabroa has a strong work ethic and a drive to succeed.

But Iluminada isn’t the only older person who continues to pursue their dreams. At 97, the world’s oldest active gymnast continues receiving medals and has no plans to retire.

They are both living proof that we can achieve our goals and dreams, no matter what challenges we may face along the way.
